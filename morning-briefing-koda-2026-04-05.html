OpenAI's $852B Valuation
OpenAI's latest mega round places its valuation above the GDP of nations like the Netherlands. The figure reflects investor conviction that frontier AI will underpin the next economic era, but it also raises the stakes enormously: the company must now justify a price tag that dwarfs every private tech venture before it. With coding specialists already beating GPT-5.4 by 14 points on benchmarks, competitive pressure is intensifying even as the capital floods in.
2
Hormuz Crisis Spikes Oil
Oil prices neared $124 after Trump's 48-hour ultimatum to Iran collided with fears of a full Strait of Hormuz blockade, through which roughly 20% of the world's petroleum transits daily. Iran's approval of a humanitarian passage offered a narrow diplomatic off-ramp, but markets are not buying the de-escalation narrative yet. The energy shock is compounding an already fragile macro environment and feeding the extreme fear reading across global indices.
3
Specialized Models Challenge Frontiers
Coding-focused AI models outperformed GPT-5.4 by 14 points on competitive programming benchmarks, signaling that domain-tuned specialists can punch well above their weight class. Meanwhile, Microsoft's Copilot added multi-model workflows and a new Cowork agent, and Mustafa Suleyman revealed that a 10-person team built MAI-Transcribe inside Microsoft. The trend is clear: smaller, targeted efforts are carving out territory that massive general-purpose models cannot easily defend.

Iran authorized ships carrying humanitarian and essential goods, including livestock feed, to transit the Strait of Hormuz under designated protocols. Industry associations have begun instructing member companies to resume sending cargo ships to southern Iranian ports. Source →

Senegal banned non-essential foreign trips for government officials as the Hormuz crisis sends petroleum import costs soaring.
