Conflict

Iranian Drones Strike Bahrain Energy Infrastructure

Iranian drone strikes hit Bahrain on April 5, igniting an oil tank fire at a Bapco Energies storage facility and severely damaging two power plants and desalination units. Bahrain's electricity and water ministry confirmed the attacks, which mark a significant expansion of Iranian targeting against Gulf Arab states in the widening 2026 Iran war. Source →

Conflict

Iran Claims U.S. C-130 Shot Down Over Isfahan

Iranian state media reported that a U.S. C-130 transport aircraft was shot down over Isfahan province during a search-and-rescue mission for a missing American airman. The incident, part of the ongoing 2026 U.S. pilot rescue operation in Iran, follows the downing of a U.S. F-15 reported on April 4 and represents a second major aircraft loss in days. Source →

Conflict

Massive Weekend Strikes Hit Iran's Petrochemical Heartland

Widespread waves of strikes continued across Iran over the April 3-4 weekend, targeting Tehran air defenses, ballistic missile arrays, and weapons R&D sites. A large-scale strike hit Iran's largest petrochemical complex in Mahshahr, responsible for approximately 70% of domestic gasoline supply. The Bushehr nuclear power plant area was struck for the fourth time, prompting Russia to begin evacuating 198 citizens working at the facility. Source →

Economy

OPEC+ Boosts Output 206,000 Barrels Amid Crisis

OPEC+ increased oil production quotas by 206,000 barrels per day for the second consecutive month, responding to energy infrastructure damage and maritime disruptions caused by the Strait of Hormuz crisis. The move aims to ease global supply pressure as oil prices remain elevated near multi-year highs. Source →

Economy

Nepal Cuts Workweek to Two Days Over Fuel

Nepal reduced government and school operations to just two days per week as the global fuel crisis triggered by the 2026 Iran war forces drastic conservation measures. The decision follows similar austerity steps by Bangladesh and Senegal, illustrating how the Hormuz disruption is cascading into developing economies far from the conflict zone. Source →

Hezbollah claimed it struck an Israeli military vessel off Lebanon's coast on April 5, while an Israeli airstrike killed five people in Kfar Hatta in Lebanon's South Governorate.
