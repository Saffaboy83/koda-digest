NovaVoice: Control Apps and Dictate With a Single Voice Layer

Unified voice control that merges smart dictation, an AI assistant, and app automation into one hands-free interface. Named a top Product Hunt product for April 2026.

Try NovaVoice

gavel The Verdict

NovaVoice is built for professionals who juggle desk work and fieldwork and need a single voice layer to handle dictation, app navigation, and workflow automation without touching a keyboard. The cloud-based unified communications backbone is solid, and the cross-platform reach (desktop clients, mobile apps, WebRTC) means you can stay connected from almost anywhere. However, the opaque pricing and enterprise-leaning feature set mean solo users and small teams should request a demo before committing; this tool rewards organizations that invest the time to map their workflows to voice commands.

Key Features

cloud

Cloud-Based Phone Services

Your entire phone system lives in the cloud. No PBX hardware to maintain, no on-site infrastructure to manage. Deployment is fast and scaling up means adding licenses, not wiring new lines.

mic

Smart Dictation Layer

Dictate emails, notes, and documents using a single voice interface that works across apps. The AI layer handles context switching so you do not need to stop and restart between tools.

devices

Cross-Platform Reach

Native clients for PC, Mac, iOS, and Android, plus WebRTC for browser-based access. One phone number follows you across every device without forwarding rules or extra configuration.

smart_toy

AI Assistant + Voice Commands

Trigger automations, navigate workflows, and control apps entirely by voice. The AI assistant interprets natural language commands and maps them to actions across your connected tools.

groups

Real-Time Collaboration

Audio conferencing, video conferencing, instant messaging, and presence indicators are bundled into the Premium tier. Teams can see who is available and jump into conversations without switching platforms.

support_agent

Auto Attendant

Even the Basic tier includes an auto attendant for call routing. Incoming calls get directed to the right person or department without a human receptionist, which is useful for lean teams.

extension

Hardware Integrations

Certified integrations with Polycom and Yealink desk phones, plus the GENBAND/Kandy platform backbone. If you already have compatible hardware, the transition is straightforward.

money_off

No Upfront Costs

NovaVoice emphasizes zero upfront hardware or maintenance costs. The subscription model means predictable monthly spend, though you will need to contact sales to learn what that spend actually is.

Who Should Use This

business SMBs Replacing Legacy Phone Systems

If your business still runs on-premise PBX hardware and you want to move to the cloud without a massive migration project, NovaVoice's zero-hardware approach and certified phone integrations make the switch relatively painless.

engineering Field + Desk Hybrid Workers

Contractors, consultants, and sales reps who split time between a desk and client sites benefit most from the voice command layer. Dictate notes from a job site, trigger CRM updates hands-free, and take calls on the same number regardless of device.

hub Teams Needing Unified Communications

Organizations tired of stitching together separate tools for voice, video, messaging, and presence. The Premium tier consolidates all of these into one platform, which reduces vendor sprawl and simplifies IT management.

accessibility_new Accessibility-Focused Workflows

Professionals with mobility limitations or repetitive strain injuries who need hands-free computing will find the single voice layer genuinely useful. Dictation plus app control in one interface removes the need to constantly switch between keyboard and voice tools.

Limitations

  • warning Opaque pricing. Every plan requires contacting sales for a quote. There is no self-serve signup, no free trial listed on the site, and no ballpark figures. This makes it impossible to compare costs against competitors like RingCentral, Dialpad, or Zoom Phone without investing time in a sales call first.
  • warning Internet dependency. As a fully cloud-based system, NovaVoice requires a stable internet connection for all functionality. If your field locations have spotty connectivity, voice quality and command responsiveness will suffer. There is no mention of offline fallback or local caching.
  • warning Limited third-party integrations. The listed integrations are hardware-focused (Polycom, Yealink) and platform-level (GENBAND/Kandy). There is no mention of integrations with popular SaaS tools like Salesforce, Slack, HubSpot, or Zapier. If you need voice commands to trigger actions in those ecosystems, you may hit a wall.
  • warning Feature gating across tiers. Collaboration features like video conferencing and instant messaging are locked behind the Premium plan. The Basic tier is quite bare, offering only voice, voicemail, and auto attendant. Teams that need both voice control and collaboration will likely need the top tier.
  • warning Sparse public documentation. The website is light on technical details about the AI assistant's capabilities, supported voice command syntax, and dictation accuracy benchmarks. For a tool that positions itself as an AI-powered voice layer, the lack of specifics is a notable gap.

Ready to go hands-free?

Start by mapping your three most repetitive daily tasks to voice commands. Measure the time saved over one week to decide if NovaVoice earns a permanent spot in your stack.
