Superhuman's AI Triage Cuts Email to Decision-Only

At $30 per month, Superhuman uses AI to auto-triage your inbox, draft replies, and surface follow-up reminders so you only touch emails that require a decision. We tested it against the noise.

open_in_new Try Superhuman
Koda Verdict

Superhuman is the best email client for professionals who receive 100+ emails daily and need to protect deep focus time. At $30/month it is not cheap, but the AI triage, split inbox, and instant reply drafts genuinely compress a 90-minute email session into 20 minutes. If email is a core part of your workflow and you are drowning, this pays for itself in the first week. If you get fewer than 50 emails a day, you probably do not need it.

Key Features

What actually matters in daily use

filter_alt

AI Auto-Triage

Incoming email is automatically classified into actionable, FYI, and noise. The AI learns your patterns over time, getting more accurate the longer you use it. Within a few days, it correctly routes about 90% of messages without intervention.

inbox

Split Inbox

Newsletters, notifications, and CC-only threads are routed out of your primary view automatically. You see only emails that require a decision or response. This is the single most impactful feature; set it up on day one.

edit_note

AI-Drafted Replies

Superhuman generates contextual reply drafts that match your writing style. They are not perfect, but they give you a solid starting point. For routine acknowledgments and scheduling replies, the drafts are often ready to send as-is.

notifications_active

Follow-Up Reminders

The system tracks sent emails and surfaces reminders when you have not received a response within a configurable window. No more manually tracking who owes you a reply. This alone prevents dozens of dropped threads per week.

keyboard

Keyboard-First Speed

Every action is mapped to a keyboard shortcut. Power users can process their entire inbox without touching the mouse. The command palette (Cmd+K) surfaces any action instantly. Processing speed is genuinely 2-3x faster than Gmail's native interface.

visibility

Read Statuses

See exactly when recipients open your emails. Useful for sales, follow-ups, and knowing whether your message was seen before you ping again. Works reliably across most email clients on the receiving end.

summarize

Instant Summaries

Long email threads are condensed into a brief summary so you can catch up without reading every message. Particularly valuable for threads with 10+ replies where you were added late. Saves significant time on cross-functional discussions.

calendar_month

Calendar Integration

View your calendar alongside email and schedule meetings directly from the compose window. Availability sharing is built in. Reduces the back-and-forth of finding meeting times, though it does not fully replace a dedicated scheduling tool.

Limitations

Where Superhuman falls short. No tool is perfect.

warning

$30/month is a hard sell for light email users. If you get fewer than 50 emails a day, Gmail with a few filters does 80% of what Superhuman offers. The value scales directly with inbox volume. Low volume means low ROI.

warning

Gmail and Google Workspace only (with Outlook support still limited). If your organization runs on Microsoft 365, the experience is not as polished. Superhuman was built Gmail-first, and it shows in the edge cases with Outlook accounts.

warning

No offline mode worth mentioning. The desktop and mobile apps require a connection for AI features. If you frequently work on planes or in areas with spotty connectivity, you will lose the triage and drafting capabilities exactly when you want to batch-process email.

warning

AI drafts still need editing for nuanced communication. For routine replies, the drafts are solid. For sensitive conversations, negotiations, or anything requiring emotional intelligence, you will rewrite most of the draft. Do not blindly send AI-generated replies to important contacts.

warning

Learning curve for the keyboard-first approach. The speed benefits only materialize after you internalize the shortcuts. Expect a week of slower-than-normal email processing before you hit your stride. The onboarding flow helps, but muscle memory takes time.

warning

Integration setup can be complex for teams. Connecting Superhuman to Salesforce, HubSpot, or Slack requires admin configuration that is not always straightforward. Solo users will not hit this issue, but team deployments should budget time for setup.
