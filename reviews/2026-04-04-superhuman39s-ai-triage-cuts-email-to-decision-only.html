Superhuman's AI Triage Cuts Email to Decision-Only

Superhuman uses AI to auto-triage your inbox, draft replies, and surface follow-up reminders so you only touch emails that require a decision. At $30/month, is aggressive inbox filtering worth the premium?

open_in_new Try It
Editorial Verdict

Superhuman is the best email client for professionals who receive 100+ emails daily and need to protect deep work time. The AI triage is genuinely useful; it correctly routes newsletters, notifications, and low-priority threads out of your primary view, leaving only messages that demand your judgment. At $30/month, it is expensive compared to free alternatives like Gmail's native filters, but the time savings are real if email volume is your bottleneck. If you get fewer than 50 emails a day, the free tier or a simpler tool will serve you fine. If your inbox is a warzone, this pays for itself in the first week.

Key Features

What actually matters when you open the app every morning.

filter_alt

AI Email Triage

The core feature. Superhuman's AI classifies incoming mail and routes newsletters, notifications, and FYI threads into separate split inbox views. Your primary inbox shows only messages that need a decision or reply. Set this up on day one.

edit_note

AI Draft Replies

Generates contextual reply drafts based on the email thread. You review, edit, and send. It handles routine acknowledgments well. Complex negotiations still need your voice, but the first draft saves real time.

notifications_active

Follow-Up Reminders

Surfaces emails where you are waiting on a reply. No more manually tracking who owes you a response. The system detects unanswered threads and nudges you at configurable intervals.

keyboard

Keyboard-First Design

Every action has a shortcut. Power users can process their entire inbox without touching the mouse. The learning curve is about a week, but the speed gains compound daily.

calendar_month

Calendar Integration

Google Calendar lives inside the sidebar. You can check availability and schedule meetings without leaving the email client. Reduces context switching between tabs.

visibility

Read Receipts and Tracking

Pro tier only. See when recipients open your emails. Useful for sales follow-ups and time-sensitive outreach. Some people find this creepy; others find it essential. Your call.

smartphone

Mobile App

Full-featured iOS and Android apps that mirror the desktop experience. The split inbox and AI triage work identically on mobile, which matters when you are triaging on the go.

analytics

Advanced Analytics

Pro tier. Tracks your response times, email volume trends, and productivity patterns. Useful for managers who want to quantify communication overhead across their team.

Limitations

No tool is perfect. Here is what you should know before committing.

warning

It is only email

Superhuman does one thing. It does not replace Slack, your project management tool, or your CRM. If you need a unified communication hub, this is not it. You are paying $30/month for an email client, and nothing else.

warning

Integrations are necessary for full value

The calendar sidebar, Slack notifications, and Zoom scheduling only work if you connect those services. Out of the box, Superhuman is a fast email client. The "productivity platform" experience requires setup and third-party accounts.

warning

$30/month is steep for light email users

If you get 20 emails a day, Gmail with a few filters does the job. The AI triage shines at scale. Below 50 daily emails, the ROI is questionable. The free tier helps, but the best features (tracking, analytics) are locked behind Pro.

warning

AI drafts still need human review

The AI-generated replies are a starting point, not a finished product. For sensitive or nuanced conversations, you will rewrite most of the draft. Do not expect to fully automate your replies.

warning

No Microsoft Exchange native support

Superhuman works best with Gmail and Google Workspace. If your organization runs on Outlook/Exchange, the experience is less polished and some features may not work as expected.
