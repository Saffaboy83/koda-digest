Trump Raises China Tariffs to 125%

President Trump escalated the trade war by raising tariffs on Chinese goods to 125%, citing Beijing's 'lack of respect,' while simultaneously announcing a 90-day pause at 10% for over 75 non-retaliating countries. Markets across Japan, India, and the Middle East tumbled as much as 8%. Goldman Sachs raised its U.S. recession probability in response to the widening trade conflict. Source →

Humanitarian

Trump Slashes $1.3B in World Food Aid

The Trump administration cut $1.3 billion in World Food Program funding covering emergency assistance to 14 famine-affected nations. WFP officials called the decision a 'death sentence for millions,' noting it contradicts prior commitments made by Secretary of State Marco Rubio. The cuts come as global food insecurity is compounded by energy price volatility from the Iran conflict and ongoing trade disruptions. Source →

Ukrainian negotiators are heading to Washington for talks next week on a proposed exchange: U.S. weapons and military support in return for American access to Ukraine's critical mineral reserves. Economy

Russia Profits From Iran Crisis Oil Surge

Russia's economy received a windfall from the oil price spike that preceded the U.S.-Iran ceasefire, with Brent crude reaching $117 per barrel before plunging 16% to below $100 after the truce was announced.
